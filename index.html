Jacob's Unofficial Guide to [Fonts]

If you have ever written anything with an electronic device, you probably have seen the numerous amount of font options that are available. What is the need of so many? Fonts can be utilized to set tone and direct the audience to a certain part of the text. Here are 3 examples of how fonts are used to do so.

  1. Comic Sans
  2. Times New Roman
  3. Roboto

Comic Sans

Comic Sans is a sans-serif font created by Vincent Connare and released by Microsoft Corporation in 1994. The font drew inspiration from comic book style lettering, depicting informal and cartoon style tone of speech bubbles.



Times New Roman

Times New Roman is a serif font used by the British newspaper The Times(italizies this) in 1931. As you most likely are aware of, it has become one of the most popualr fonts used of all time, beign the standard for most personal computers because of its simplisitc and formal style.



Roboto

Roboto is a font released by Google in 2011. It creates a neutral, human tone that can be read as casual and approachable.
